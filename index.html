Record narration to file
Use your OS audio recorder — no extra software needed.
  1. Open QuickTime Player → File → New Audio Recording.
  2. Click the arrow next to record → set input to system output. BlackHole (free, existential.audio) gives clean loopback with no mic bleed.
  3. Press record in QuickTime, then click Open narration view & play below.
  4. When narration ends, stop QuickTime and save as M4A or convert to MP3 via VLC.
No BlackHole? Hold your phone near the speaker and use Voice Memos.
  1. Press Win+G → Xbox Game Bar → ⬤ Start recording (or Win+Alt+R).
  2. Click Open narration view & play below.
  3. Press Win+Alt+R when done. File saves to Videos\Captures. Extract audio with VLC → Media → Convert.
Better option: Audacity → WASAPI loopback → exports directly to MP3.
  1. Open Audacity → set recording device to Monitor of [output] (PulseAudio).
  2. Press Record, then click Open narration view & play below.
  3. When done, stop Audacity and export as MP3 or OGG.
CLI: parecord --monitor-stream=0 output.wav
